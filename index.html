How the money works
in 5 flight compensation cases

This is an example based on 5 cases:
  • the average value of one case is £520;
  • all 5 cases are sent to LBA (letter before action);
  • 1 of them (non-Ryanair) is positive — the airline pays after the letter;
  • 4 of them go to court (2 non-Ryanair and 2 Ryanair);
  • 2 of them are won (1 non-Ryanair and 1 Ryanair) and 2 are lost.

What happens month by month

M1
Start: all 5 cases go to the lawyers

Skycop hands all 5 cases over to APS, and APS sends the airline 5 letters before action (LBA). Roland lends £143 for the solicitor's work and office needs.

Roland pays £143
M3
Answers to the letters

In Case 1 (non-Ryanair) the airline agrees to pay after the letter. Cases 2–5 go to court (2 non-Ryanair and 2 Ryanair) — court fees are due: 4 × £70 = £280. Roland lends this money too.

Roland pays £280
M5
First money: Case 1

The airline pays £520. The passenger receives £260, Skycop keeps £260 and starts repaying Roland.

Case 1 — won by LBA
M12
Court, part one

Case 2 is won: £520 compensation, plus the court returns the £70 fee and £70 of legal costs. Case 3 is lost: its £70 fee is gone.

Case 2 — won Case 3 — lost
M14
Court, part two. The finish

Case 4 is won (the same £520 + 70 + 70), Case 5 is lost (£70 fee gone). Skycop settles with Roland in full — with interest and the financing fee.

Case 4 — won Case 5 — lost
